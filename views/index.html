Laying the foundation to enquire into the world

The senses are the windows to the world. Treated with respect, they are capacities through which we develop strong faculties. This stunning alphabet set and delightful book of poems reinforce early childhood learning and extends vocabulary, while encouraging and developing the comprehension of art and literature in a fun and exciting way.

Foster the senses and make the most of life!

26 beautiful alphabet cards together with a book of poems fire all sorts of synapses in the brain, stimulating imagination.

The set includes beautiful paintings of the things in our world that our children can point to, or see and describe.

To hear a child describe a butterfly as a 'fluttering rainbow',

a volcano as 'scary exciting, mountain on fire',

or ask an elaphant, 'can I be your friend?',

is one of the gifts this alphabet set offers like no other.

The set includes concepts such as 'under', 'quiet', or 'touch'

The poem envokes the touch sense, an internal response to contact with the outside world.
The image suggests a greater idea where a tiny touch or hint of something can bring out special wonder

'Idea': "in my head an idea I see..."

In my head, an idea I see. Would you like to try with me?
A square and a triangle joined up as one! I like making shapes, it's lots of fun.

And 'quiet': "...shhhhh...",

Quiet night, no sound
Dreams inside my head float around
Silent stars, still air
Peaceful quiet
Everywhere
Shhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhhh

I is for 'igloo' or Q for 'queen' are not as relevant as they once were.
It is delight to take the letter 'u' for 'under' and watch a 3-year-old cheekily tuck it under the 'car': "toot toot chomp!"

alphabet art is a set of 26 stunning alphabet picture cards to be hung in any configuration, plus a delightful book of poems for bedtime reading. Instructions for hanging the cards is included (such as using a 3M removable adhesive, using strips of felt and Velcro, using pegs).

Recommended

alphabet art is recommended as a gift to an expecting family for decorating the nursery, and for any child under six. As a gift to a nursing mother it is such a divine set of gorgeous images to contemplate while nursing and adds warmth and love to the room. When given to a toddler, it is loved by both girls and boys.

Loved by girls

Early readers engage immediately with the poems and like to trace their finger down the side of each page where the whole alphabet is printed and also tracing the letter on the picture.

Loved by boys

You can be confidently assured the pictures especially will be really valued, and thus a great entry to the use of descriptive language.

Loved by boys

My experience over the past 12 years allows me to tell you alphabet art will stay on the wall when other imagery come down. I have been visited at fairs by 14 - 16 year old children (both boys and girls) who were gifted a set in their toddler-hood and they tell me they still have alphabet art on their bedroom walls, and remain, well, basically in love with it!

About

The alphabet set was originally painted by Austin Whincup as a gift to his brother and sister-in-law on the birth of their child. Austin was working as a dishwasher in London at the time, so collected plastic lids from the ice-cream containers, and with the guidance of his mother back home in New Zealand (early childhood teacher Joan), he would swap his gloves for his paintbrushes completed this marvellous alphabet and sent it home where it was immediately hung in the new baby’s room.
Many friends wanted a copy and one in particular, Moira Wairama, a Wellington poet and playwright who also taught early childhood in Aotearoa, pretty much insisted she write a poem for each letter. In doing so, “Alphabet Art” was born.
